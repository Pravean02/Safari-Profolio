Safari: An AI-Driven Sri Lankan Tour Guide

An AI-powered computerized platform for tour guide booking that enhances trust, privacy, and intelligent matching through an LLM-based agent architecture.

Learn More

About The Project

Our Vision

To revolutionize the Sri Lankan tourism landscape by bridging the gap between international tourists and local guides through secure, context-aware, and intelligent digital interactions that prioritize safety and personalization.

Institutional Support

This research is proudly conducted at the Sri Lanka Institute of Information Technology (SLIIT), Malabe campus, leveraging academic excellence to solve real-world industry challenges within the Department of Computer Systems Engineering.

Research Domain

1. Background

Tourism plays a critical role in the overall Sri Lankan economy, competing successfully due to its pivotal geographical position. Travelers are increasingly relying on online platforms to plan itineraries and connect with tour guides.

2. Research Gap

Existing digital platforms heavily rely on static filtering methods and lack adequate verification processes, exposing tourists to risks. Furthermore, the absence of a privacy-aware workflow leads to sharing personal contact details too early.

3. Research Problem

Current marketplace platforms fail to provide dynamic, intelligent personalization while simultaneously ensuring stringent, multi-layered identity verification and data privacy for tourists and local guides.

4. Research Objectives

To propose and develop an AI-powered tour guide booking platform that enhances trust, privacy, and intelligent matching using conversational requirement elicitation, detail-locking, and government-issued document verification.

Research Features

Conversational AI Agent

Integration of a Google Gemini-powered agent to perform contextual itinerary recommendations and provide bid-assisted guide matching through voice or text.

Privacy-Aware Workflow

Implementation of a detail-locking mechanism that actively restricts the sharing of sensitive user information between tourists and guides until a booking is officially confirmed.

Multi-Layered Verification

A rigorous three-step Know Your Customer (KYC) workflow ensuring guide authenticity through the validation of national identity cards, tourist board licenses, and police clearance checks.
